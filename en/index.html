Frequently Asked Questions

Is Keyly still worth it if I do not use AI very often?+
Absolutely. The heart of Keyly is its high-performance Zhuyin engine, built for speed, precision, and clean correction. Even without AI, it is designed to deliver a sharp, fluid typing experience on iOS.
Which languages does Keyly support?+
We focus heavily on the best possible Traditional Chinese Zhuyin experience while fully supporting mixed Chinese and English input. On top of that, Keyly's AI can help with multilingual translation and refinement in one tap. AI-generated content should still be reviewed for formal use.
Why does installation require “Allow Full Access”?+

This is the technical requirement that allows a third-party keyboard on iOS to connect to cloud AI services.

If you leave it off: You can still keep using Keyly's core Zhuyin engine forever, and supported devices can still use basic on-device AI features fully offline.

If you turn it on: We process data only for the immediate AI request, then remove it from cache. Keyly does not monitor, record, or store your private everyday conversations.

Which cloud AI providers do you use, and is it safe?+
We build on top-tier providers including Google, OpenAI, and Anthropic. Keyly strictly limits data use to real-time requests and does not allow your personal conversations to be used for training. Automatic cache clearing helps ensure data is removed after each task completes.
Can I still use AI refinement without internet access?+
Yes, if your device supports Apple's on-device AI capabilities. On supported hardware, Keyly can perform local AI refinement offline, giving you both faster response times and stronger privacy guarantees.
Will fast typing cause lag or crashes?+
We have a high bar for performance. Keyly is tuned by an experienced iOS engineering team and tested across multiple device profiles to keep the typing flow stable, smooth, and responsive even at high speed.
How are my custom AI commands stored?+
Your custom prompts are currently stored locally on your device using encryption. We are also planning encrypted cloud sync so that future device switching can be much more seamless.
What is the music keyboard, or Piano Mode?+
It is a built-in easter egg that adds high-quality piano tones to your typing. You can turn it on or off whenever you want and make even routine replies feel a little more alive.
What is the difference between the one-time purchase and Keyly Pro?+

One-time purchase (limited-time NT$90): Includes the high-speed Zhuyin engine, the standard prompt library, and the current daily quota of 5 cloud AI refinements.

Keyly Pro (NT$150/month): Built for heavy users who want unlimited cloud AI usage plus the AI Prompt Manager for creating, editing, and saving custom commands.
