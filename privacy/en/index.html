Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 28, 2026

💡 Summary: Keyly processes data only to provide account, subscription, and cloud AI features that you actively use. We do not automatically upload your everyday typing just because you installed or use the keyboard.

1. Overview

Keyly is an iOS third-party keyboard and AI-assisted writing app. It includes an on-device typing experience, a Sign in with Apple account system, optional cloud AI features, and subscription and quota management.

We follow a data minimization approach. We do not collect keyboard input for advertising, and we do not upload ordinary typing content to our servers unless you actively use a feature that requires it. When you use account, subscription, or cloud AI features, we do process the data needed to provide those services.

If you do not use account, billing, or cloud AI features, most of the Keyly typing and settings experience can remain on-device.

2. Categories of Data We Process

2.1 Account and Authentication Data

When you create or sign in to an account with Sign in with Apple, we may process:

  • Apple-provided sign-in credentials and identifier information
  • Your Keyly account identifier
  • Authentication sessions and access token / refresh token data
  • Account deletion requests and deletion scheduling status

This data is used for sign-in, account security, session management, and account deletion flows.

2.2 Subscription, Purchase, and Quota Data

If you purchase a Keyly subscription or quota package, we may process transaction information needed for App Store verification, your current subscription status and renewal timing, and AI quota-related records.

2.3 Cloud AI Request Data

When you actively use a cloud AI feature, the system processes the text you submit and the request context needed to generate a result. This data is used only when you explicitly trigger cloud AI functionality and is not retained as general keyboard behavior tracking data.

If you use only local features or on-device AI modes, the related content does not need to go through the cloud AI path.

2.4 Local Settings and Personalization Data

Keyly stores necessary local data on your device, such as keyboard and AI preferences, settings shared between the app and the keyboard extension, personal dictionary or learning results, and local caches and session state.

2.5 Diagnostics, Analytics, and Remote Configuration Data

Keyly currently uses Firebase Analytics, Firebase Remote Config, and Supabase to support product operations. These services may process data related to product behavior, feature flags, reliability, diagnostics, or service security. We do not send keyboard text content as general analytics events.

3. How We Use Data

  • To create and maintain your account
  • To verify session state and protect account security
  • To provide cloud AI features
  • To verify subscription, quota, and payment entitlements
  • To store your settings and personalization preferences
  • To prevent abuse, keep the service reliable, and troubleshoot problems
  • To fulfill account deletion and legal obligations

4. What We Do Not Do

  • We do not sell your personal data for advertising purposes
  • We do not collect keyboard input as general tracking data without your action
  • We do not automatically upload everything you type just because you installed the keyboard

5. Third-Party Services

Keyly may use the following third-party services or platforms to process necessary data:

  • Apple: sign-in, App Store subscriptions, and transaction flows
  • Supabase: accounts, database, edge functions, and related backend capabilities
  • Firebase: analytics and remote configuration
  • Cloud AI providers and infrastructure: only when you actively use cloud AI features

Each third-party service handles data under its own policies and practices.

6. Data Retention

  • Local settings and personalization data: generally remain on your device until you delete the app, clear data, or reset settings
  • Account, subscription, quota, and security-related data: retained for as long as needed to provide the service
  • Legal, tax, transaction, dispute-resolution, or security records: may be retained longer where required

7. Account Deletion

You can submit an account deletion request inside the app. Once the request is accepted:

  • Your account is immediately deactivated and signed out
  • A 30-day grace period begins
  • During those 30 days, you cannot sign in again or start over with the same Apple ID
  • After 30 days, we delete the account and deletable data
  • You immediately lose access to Keyly cloud AI, subscription-linked entitlements, and related account-based services

Deleting your Keyly account does not automatically cancel your App Store subscription. You still need to cancel it yourself on the Apple subscription management page. Subscription billing and refund decisions remain subject to Apple's policies and handling.

Where required for legal obligations, tax, payment verification, dispute handling, or security purposes, we may retain limited data for the necessary period instead of deleting everything immediately.

8. Your Rights

Depending on the laws that apply to you, you may have rights to access, correct, delete eligible data, or object to specific processing activities.

9. Children's Privacy

Where required by applicable law, children should use services involving accounts, payments, or cloud features only with parent or guardian consent.

10. Changes to This Policy

If we update this policy, we may provide notice in the app, on the App Store page, or through other appropriate channels when required, and we will update the last updated date of this document.

11. Contact

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy or the account deletion flow, please contact us at support@keylyapp.com.
